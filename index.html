Q.01What stack do I really need to already know?+
At minimum: 3+ months writing Python (functions, classes, testing) and SQL (joins, aggregations, window functions). We don't cover "what is a for-loop." Week 1 starts at the level you'd find in an analytics-engineer JD.
Q.02How much time per week is this?+
Plan for 12-15 hours/week: 4-6 hours of live sessions, the rest on capstone build + PR iterations. It's faster than a bootcamp but more intense than a course. If you're working full-time, talk to us about a part-time variant before applying.
Q.03Is this placement-guaranteed?+
No — we don't sell fake guarantees. We can tell you Cohort 02 had 80% placement within 90 days of demo day, at a median uplift of ₹6.2L. But "guaranteed placement" is the language of low-quality programs. We're honest about outcomes because the work itself sells.
Q.04Will I get a certificate?+
You'll get a GitHub portfolio that does what a certificate never will — shows the work. We do issue a signed "completion" certificate, but we explicitly tell every recruiter who reaches out that the proof is the repo, not the PDF.
Q.05What if I don't get selected?+
We reject about 60% of applicants — usually because they're earlier in their journey than the cohort needs. We're explicit about why in the rejection email, and we'll tell you exactly what to come back with for Cohort 04.
Q.06Why only 10 seats?+
Because the senior reviewers can only sustain 10 PRs/week without burning out. The number is the bottleneck on quality. When we can expand the review board sustainably, the cohort will grow.
Q.07I'm outside India — can I join?+
Yes, but pricing changes (USD invoice) and session times shift to IST-friendly slots. Email us first — current cohort has 2 residents from Singapore and one from Lagos.
